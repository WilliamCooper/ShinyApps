Info for PSXC plots

[esc to dismiss, or click the button at the bottom]

This tab tests PSXC by assuming that the avionics pressure PS_A has been consistent for all projects. If that is the case, a prediction of PSXC based on PS_A and QC_A should test for consistency in a new project. A fit to “good” projects (at present, DEEPWAVE, CSET, ORCAS) led to a typical prediction for PSXC on the basis of PS_A and QC_A, called PSFIT, that has residual standard deviation from PSXC of about 0.2 hPa. This is smaller than our estimated uncertainty in PSXC, so this formula provides a stringent prediction of PSXC that should be able to detect any significant error in new projects.

The drop-down menu, flight number, and “All” checkbox in the top panel allow selection of any flight in the data archives. The “All” checkbox requires reading all the data files for a given project, so it will take longer (up to a minute or so before plots will appear).

The results for the three reference projects are shown in the plots below. Significant departure from these may indicate a problem with the measurement PSXC. For these results, it is assumed that the standard PCOR correction, based on LAMS, has already been applied.

The measurements entering these plots have some restrictions applied to avoid large departures that are usually not indicative of real problems. The restrictions are: rate of climb between -1 and 1 m/s (because PS_A has delayed response), TASX > 90 (to avoid possible periods with flaps), and the rate of change in TASX < 0.1 m/s per second (also to avoid problems with the slow response of PS_A).

The standard deviation in the first plot will normally be about 0.3 hPa or smaller, and the mean should be within about 0.2 hPa of zero. The second plot shows the altitude dependence of the difference, with the number on the right showing the number of measurements included at each level in the plot. Larger deviations are expected where there is a small number of observations. However, systematic departures from the standard plots that follow may indicate problems that should be investigated. No error bar is displayed if the limits would extend beyond the range of the plot.
