Checking Humidity Measurements: Recommendations

  1. Start with the "Review" tab; see plots 5--8. Use "brush" to zoom to problems. Look for general consistency with past projects.

  2. Use the "Known Problems" -> "DP overshoots/SS" tab to check for overshooting regions (usually) following rapid descents and regions where the measurements indicate significant (more than a few %) supersaturation. Consider saving flags for these regions so they can be identified later, either by setting the measurement missing or by including a quality flag for the measurement.

  3. It may be useful to check the "Known Problems" -> "in-cloud check" tab again to consider the agreement between temperature and dewpoint for measurements in cloud.

  4. The "Known Problems" -> "check DP cavity P" tab should be used to see if the cavity pressures are similar to those expected. Significant departures from the predictions based on past projects are reasons to suspect the dew point measurements.

  5. As with all measurements, it may be useful to check the "Known Problems" -> "out of range, frozen, spikes' tab also.
