Component 2:

The smallest change in an indicator that can be perceived is difficult to characterize for an analog display, but it is straightforward for a digital display. For example, consider a transducer that produces voltages in the range from 0–5 V, connected to a analog-to-digital converter that produces a 12-bit signal covering this full range. Because 212 = 4096, 4096 increments are available, so the smallest voltage difference that can be indicated is 5∕4096 ≈ 0.00122 V. This limits the resolution to not better than 1.22 mV, and that is often quoted as the resolution of the instrument. However, if the instrumental precision is larger than this, the smallest increment that can be distinguished will be larger than this value, as discussed in the component-1 section. The digitizer resolution is often a poor measure of instrumental resolution if the precision is larger than this, but many instrument specifications indicate only this resolution.
