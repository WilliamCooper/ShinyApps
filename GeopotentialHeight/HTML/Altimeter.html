The Altimeter Setting

The altimeter setting is an alternate way of interpolating from an elevated-station pressure to a surface pressure, so this discussion is complementary to the discussion of sea-level pressure in the main module. At levels below 18,000 ft (about 5486 m), aircraft use an altimeter setting reported from a nearby surface station in order to determine their altitudes. The setting is a compensation to the standard-atmosphere reading of the altimeter to ensure that, when an aircraft lands using that setting, the surface will appear at the expected elevation. Using a common altimeter setting also ensures flight levels assigned to aircraft are consistent. Without such compensation, the altimeter could indicate serious errors when the atmosphere differs from standard-atmosphere stratification.

The diagram in the right panel illustrates the relationships among the station pressure (ps), station altitude (zs), and altimeter setting. The default case considered has a station elevation of 1350 m and station pressure of 885 hPa. The left axis shows the standard-atmosphere relationship between altitude and pressure for a portion of the atmosphere below 2 km. Also shown are the assumed station elevation (red dot labeled “zs”) and station pressure (blue dot labeled “ps”). If these were at corresponding positions on the axis, the altimeter setting would be that for the standard atmosphere (29.92 inHg). Because they are not matched, the way to get agreement between the station pressure and station elevation is to move the pressure scale upward along the height scale until the station pressure matches the station elevation. Once those match, the pressure on the right scale at sea level (here 1040.3 hPa) is the altimeter setting. (Altimeter settings always have units of inches of mercury, so this needs to be converted to 1040.3*29.92/1013.25≈30.72 inHg. As discussed in the section on units, this unit would be best avoided if possible. It is unfortunate that it is thoroughly embedded in aviation instruments.) Altimeters used on aircraft adjust their displayed altitude-vs-pressure in just this way, adding or subtracting a fixed altitude at all pressures.

You can use the interactive display in the right panel to find the altimeter setting for other conditions. Enter the station pressure and elevation into the top two entry slots, and then adjust the altimeter-setting slider until the adjusted station pressure displayed with the right-axis values matches the station elevation. The slider then displays the altimeter setting in units of inHg, while the blue number at the bottom of the right axis shows the equivalent value in hPa.
