Follow-up Questions:

  1. Suppose measurements from a GPS receiver on a research aircraft, flying at latitude 60N, indicate that the geometric altitude above sea level is 12000 m when the pressure is 200 hPa. What is the geopotential height, and how different is this from the international standard atmosphere? [A: geopotential height is 11992.67 m; standard atmosphere would be 11784.05 at 200 mb]

  2. If the aircraft is maintaining flight at 200 hPa, is it above or below the nominal geometric altitude that would correspond to its geopotential altitude? [A: well above, by about 216 m.]

  3. What does this departure tell you about the temperature of the column of air below the aircraft (i.e., is the appropriately weighted average above or below that of the standard atmosphere)? [A: A warmer atmosphere requires integration of the hydrostatic equation to a greater final height to achieve a specified pressure, so in this case the atmosphere is warmer than the standard atmosphere.]
