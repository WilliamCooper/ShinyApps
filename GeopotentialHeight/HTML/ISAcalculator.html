Using the ISA calculator

The International Standard Atmosphere is shown in the plots to the right, showing how pressure and temperature vary with height. The red dots in the two panels denote the tropopause (separating the troposphere and the stratosphere) and the stratopause (separing the stratosphere and the mesosphere). The plots and these calculators assume dry air without water vapor.

To obtain specific values, enter a pressure in the top left entry slot, and the corresponding geopotential height will be displayed below that line. Enter the pressure altitude (which is a geopotential altitude) in the next entry slot, and the corresponding pressure will be displayed below that line.
