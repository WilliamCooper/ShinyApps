Using the Demonstration

The plot to the right shows the flow angle represented by a specified angle of attack and the pressure, temperature, and airspeed where the measurement is made. You can adjust either the angle-of-attack slider or the pressure-difference slider (representing the pressure difference between the upward- and downward-oriented ports), and you can study how the ambient pressure and temperature and the airspeed affect these results. In addition, the pressure difference between the forward port and the ports on the side of the cylinder is shown at the bottom of the left panel.

Notice these features:

  1. Once pressure, temperature, and airspeed are specified, the dynamic pressure is determined. However, the pressure difference displayed at the bottom of the left panel still changes with angle of attack because, when the forward port is not directly forward, the pressure difference is reduced as specified by the equation in the preceding tab.

  2. By convention, angle of attack is positive for air moving upward relative to the sensor. Angle of attack and the pressure difference between the top and bottom ports are therefore negatively correlated.

  3. The pressure differences increase as the square of the airspeed, making the angle measurements more sensitive to pressure as the airspeed increases. However, reducing ambient pressure reduces the same sensitivity and so leads to larger uncertainties at upper flight levels for a given uncertainty in the measurement of the differential pressure.
