Adiabatic Processes

A process that occurs without the transfer of heat or mass across the boundaries of the system is called adiabatic. If a confined gas in a cylinder is compressed by the motion of a piston while the cylinder resides in a reservoir that maintains its temperature, that process is not adiabatic because heat will be transferred from the cylinder to the reservoir. However, equalization of pressure that occurs when a valve separating two chambers is opened is adiabatic. When a buoyant parcel (i.e., one having density lower than its surroundings) rises in the atmosphere, the process in the core of the parcel can be approximated as adiabatic, so relationships for adiabatic processes are useful when one considers stability in the atmosphere. An adiabatic process can still include work done on or by the system, where the work is equal to the pressure times the change in volume.
