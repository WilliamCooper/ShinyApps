The Standard Atmosphere

The US Standard Atmosphere is defined in the troposphere by a reference pressure and temperature of pR =1013.25 hPa and TR = 15˚C and a specified lapse rate of λ=-0.0065˚C /m or -6.5˚C /km. The definition also specifies that Rd=287.0531 J kg-1 K-1 and g=9.80665 m/s2 should be used. These formulas apply until the temperature drops to 216.65 K, at which point different stratification is assumed to apply in the stratosphere.

The standard atmosphere has many important uses in meteorology. For example, on constant-pressure weather maps like the 500-hPa chart , pressure is plotted as the departure in height from that expected at 500 hPa in the standard atmosphere. In the US, aircraft fly on pressure-altitude surfaces when above 18,000 ft.
