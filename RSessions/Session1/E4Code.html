Exercise: Estimate what change in recovery factor would be needed to bring DEEPWAVE temperature measurements ATHR1 and ATRL from RF20 into agreement.



The recovery factor ( ) is used to correct from the direct measurements (in this data file, RTHR1 and RTRL) to obtain measurements of ambient temperature: If V is the airspeed (TASX) and Cp the specific heat at constant pressure (1005 in SI units), then

where denote the average value

So this change in the difference between the recovery factors will lead to mean ambient temperature measurements that are the same.



The code that generates the answer is shown below:

## DEX4 <- getNetCDF (“/scr/raf_data/DEEPWAVE/DEEPWAVErf20.nc”, c(“ATHR1”, “ATRL”, “RTHR1”, “RTRL”, “TASX”))

## save (DEX4, file=”Session1/DEX4.Rdata”)

load (“DEX4.Rdata”)

## restrict to times with TASX > 80:

DEX4 <- DEX4[DEX4$TASX > 80, ]

CP <- 1005

with (DEX4, Dalpha <<- mean ((RTHR1-RTRL) * CP / TASX^2, na.rm=TRUE))

print (sprintf (“The required change in the difference between recovery factors would be %.3f”, Dalpha))

The 5th line illustrates how to create a subset of the data satisfying some test, in this case that the airspeed be above 80; the “, ]” at the end includes all the variables in the data.frame at times that meet this test. The line starting “with ...” illustrates extraction of the variables from the data.frame without requiring the full syntax like DEX4$RTHR1 for each variable; <<- denotes extraction to the environment outside the “with” statement so the result can be printed.



The answer obtained is -0.010 for the adjustment that would be required in the difference between recovery factors to bring the two temperatures into mean agreement.
