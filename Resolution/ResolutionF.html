Summary

The recommended definition of resolution is, “the smallest difference between measurands that will result in measurements indicating that the measurands are different." With this definition, if a level of confidence corresponding to one standard deviation (about 68.3%) is desired, the resolution of an instrument or sensor is about twice the precision with which the instrument makes measurements. Use the “Probability Distributions, Meaning #1” tab with the first slider set to 2 and the second selection to "68.3%" to see this case. If the measurement is encoded by an analog-to-digital converter, the resolution is further limited by the difference in measurand corresponding to a one-bit change in the digitized output, as illustrated in the “Illustration of Meaning #2” tab. This is often described as the resolution of the measurement, but this usage is incorrect unless the resolution of the digitizer is the dominant limitation on the resolution.
