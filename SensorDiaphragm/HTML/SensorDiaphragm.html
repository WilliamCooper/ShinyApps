Operating conditions:

  • reference pressure pref=1000 hPa

  • reference temperature at calibration 293.15 K

Try changing the pressure and see how the diaphragm deflects. Or enable the "pressure" animation radio button. In a pressure sensor, this mechanical motion is translated to an output, perhaps by deflection of a pointer on a dial or by detection of the stress produced on an electronic sensor.

The reading is very sensitive to temperature. Try moving the temperature slider and see how sensitive the instrument is to changes in temperature. The gas is the reference chamber obeys the ideal gas law, so with a sealed volume the pressure is proportional to temperature. The error arising from a temperature different from the reference temperature is displayed below the temperature slider. Sensitivity to temperature is a common source of error in pressure sensors.

The reading is also sensitive to the direction of the wind relative to the surface of the sensor, because downward motion of the wind increases the pressure at the entrance to the inlet tube. Explore this by varying the angle of attack and the airspeed.
