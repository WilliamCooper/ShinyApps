Using the Calculator

These calculations are based on the Murphy-Koop formulas for equilibrium vapor pressure, provided in this publication. Details as implemented in calculations for the NSF/NCAR research aircraft are available at .this URL, page 38.

Enter the dew-point temperature (in units of oC) and the atmospheric pressure (in units of hPa), and the calculator will display the equilibrium vapor pressure, mixing ratio, and specific humidity below the entry slots. Enter the vapor pressure (in units of hPa) and the calculator will display the equilibrium dew-point temperature below the entry. When a new temperature or pressure is entered in the top entry slots, this vapor pressure will be updated to match the resulting vapor pressure. The calculator also displays the molecular weight and gas constant of moist air (labeled Ma and Ra) and the mole fractions for the specified water-vapor pressure.

You can also mouse-click on the plot at the right to set the temperature.

If the "enhancement factor" checkbox is not checked, the vapor pressure displayed is that in equilibrium at the supplied dew-point temperature. If it is checked, it is assumed that the dew-point supplied is a measurement from a chilled-mirror or similar instrument operating at the specified atmospheric pressure, for which the equilibrium vapor pressure is raised by a small amount by the enhancement factor described in the above publication. The "corrected dew-point temperature" is then the temperature at which equilibrium would be present without the presence of the dry part of the atmospheric pressure.

The mixing ratio is calculated using the standard formula, and the specific humidity is then calculated using the relationship q=r/(1+r) where r is the mixing ratio.
