Additional Information and Credits

The associated shiny app is available here: https://williamcooper.shinyapps.io/VarSpec. By default, that application displays the code used, either at the right side of the display or below it, and the location can be changed by an action button with that display. The shiny app and the LyX code for this memo are available at https://github.com/WilliamCooper/ShinyApps/VarSpec.

The calculation reported here used R (R Core Team [2016]), with RStudio (RStudio [2009]), the knitr (Xie [2013, 2014]) package, and the shiny application package (Chang et al. [2017]). The Welch method of segmentation used the bspec package (Roever et al. [2011]). The spectrum()” function is part of core R as cited above and was used to generate many of the variance spectra. Data files in netCDF format have been read and written using the R package “ncdf4”; cf. Pierce [2015]. Substantial use also was made of the ggplot2 package (Wickham [2009]) for R. The R package “Ranadu” [http://github.com/WilliamCooper/Ranadu] was used extensively. The hourly surface observations are from this source: National Climatic Data Center/NESDIS/NOAA/U.S. Department of Commerce. 1983. NCDC TD14 and TD3280 U.S. Surface Airway Hourly Observations, 1938(1948)-2005. Research Data Archive at the National Center for Atmospheric Research, Computational and Information Systems Laboratory. http://rda.ucar.edu/datasets/ds470.0/. Accessed 17 Nov 2017. Additional information regarding the generation of variance spectra and especially the associated uncertainties was obtained from Childers [1978].
