1Even for velocity components, “power spectrum” seems inappropriate. The specific energy, or energy per unit mass, has units of J/kg or m2s-2 and so the units are the same as the variance in a component of the wind. In the case of a time series, the spectral variance P(ν) can be regarded as the derivative of the cumulative integral of variance over frequency contributions (CV(ν)=∫ 0νP(ν′)dν′), and for a wind component P(ν) will have units of m2s-2 per unit frequency or m2s-1. The specific power has units of specific energy per time or m2s-3 (watts per kilogram) and the specific power per frequency interval would have units of m2s-2 which differs from the units of P(ν). In the case of wind, P(ν) is better regarded as a spectral energy rather than a spectral power.
