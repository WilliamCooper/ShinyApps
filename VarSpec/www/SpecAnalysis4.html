3One might wonder if tidal variations caused by the gravitational attraction of the moon can be detected. In the ocean, tides occur approximately twice per day, with the period 12 h 25.2 min or a frequency of 1.9324 day-1. There is no sign of this peak in the middle variance spectrum, but there is enough noise arising from the variance in the spectral estimate that a signal may be hidden there. However, moon-related tides may be greater at coastal locations because the predominant effect is caused by the motion of the ocean, not the gravitational pull on the atmosphere.
